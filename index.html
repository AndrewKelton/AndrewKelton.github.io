About Me

 Hi, I'm Andrew Kelton — a Computer Science student at the University of Central Florida (UCF), graduating in December 2026. Growing up, I watched my older brother pursue CS and was inspired to follow a similar path. That early spark turned into a genuine passion for building things that solve real problems.

 My interests lie at the intersection of technology and biology — I'm fascinated by how software, AI, and data can be applied to life sciences and push the boundaries of what's medically and scientifically possible. On the technical side, I gravitate toward systems programming, machine learning, and high-performance computing. In Spring 2025 I conducted research on parallel TF-IDF text classifier implementations in C++ — you can read more about it in the Projects section below.

 Last summer I interned at Urban Sky in Denver, Colorado as an AI Systems Intern, where I designed and deployed a conversational multi-agent system used by non-technical team members to query manufacturing data and generate automated reports.

 Outside of coding, I'm a dedicated bodybuilder — the same discipline I apply to training carries over into how I approach engineering: consistently, methodically, and always pushing further.

Projects

A Parallel TF-IDF Implementation in C++

January 2025 – April 2025
GitHub ↗

A multithreaded, parallel TF-IDF vectorizer and text classifier written in C++. Computes TF-IDF scores across a training corpus, sorts documents into categories, then classifies unseen documents using cosine similarity (0.8 trained : 0.2 un-trained ratio). The first multithreaded TF-IDF implementation for efficient text classification in C++.
